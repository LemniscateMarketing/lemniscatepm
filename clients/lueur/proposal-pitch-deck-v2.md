# Lueur Skin Clinic: Strategic Partnership Proposal

**Prepared for:** Lueur (formerly Ozmedica)  
**Location:** Melbourne, Australia  
**Engagement Type:** 6-Month Strategic Partnership  
**Investment:** $8,500 AUD / month (excl. GST)  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Positioning:** Premium concise version

---

## 1. Executive Position

Lueur is at an important inflection point. The clinic is rebranding, the domain transition needs to be handled carefully, and the Melbourne aesthetics market is too competitive to rely on a basic website and manual front-desk follow-up.

The core commercial challenge is clear:

- A premium clinic needs a premium digital experience to convert trust into bookings.
- Missed calls, delayed replies, and fragmented booking workflows leak qualified leads.
- Paid media in Melbourne is expensive enough that weak conversion infrastructure becomes costly very quickly.

This engagement is designed to solve those issues as one system, not as disconnected marketing tasks.

---

## 2. Scope of Engagement

This 6-month partnership combines three coordinated workstreams:

### 2.1 Website and Digital Foundation

- Full redesign and rebuild of the Lueur website on WordPress.
- Mobile-first UX focused on consultations and bookings, not generic brochure traffic.
- Booking integration with your existing practice management platform, or a recommended alternative if needed.
- Careful migration from `ozmedica.com.au` to the new Lueur domain, including redirect mapping and search console monitoring.
- Technical SEO foundation, page structure, speed optimization, and local schema.

### 2.2 AI and Booking Infrastructure

- AI voice receptionist for after-hours coverage, overflow handling, and common enquiry triage.
- Website chatbot focused on qualification, FAQs, and booking progression.
- Automated reminder, follow-up, welcome, and re-engagement sequences.
- Human review and guardrails so automation improves conversion without creating compliance or patient-experience risk.

### 2.3 Patient Acquisition and Growth

- Local SEO and Google Business Profile optimization.
- Google Ads focused on high-intent treatment and consultation demand.
- Meta Ads focused on local awareness, retargeting, and conversion support.
- SEO content to build authority and reduce paid reliance over time.
- Organic social support with Instagram as the primary priority channel. Secondary channels should be expanded only when content supply supports them.

---

## 3. Intended Business Outcomes

By the end of this 6-month engagement, Lueur should have:

- A stronger, more premium website that reflects the clinic brand and converts better.
- Faster response coverage across calls, chat, and booking flows.
- Better lead capture from both high-intent search traffic and local awareness campaigns.
- A more compliant, measurable, and operationally mature marketing system.
- Clear reporting on what is producing consultations and where spend should scale.

The objective is not vanity metrics. The objective is stronger enquiry conversion, better operational follow-through, and more qualified consultations.

## 8. Why This Structure Is Commercially Strong

### Website Included, Not Quoted Separately

Many agencies would separate the website project into a large upfront build fee, then sell marketing on top. Here, the site is embedded inside the engagement, which makes the launch sequence tighter and lowers friction at the point of decision.

### Growth and Operations Are Treated as One System

This engagement does not stop at traffic generation. It addresses the full path from click to call to booking to follow-up, which is where many clinics lose otherwise qualified demand.

### Commercial Transparency

- clear retainer
- media billed at cost
- no hidden website build fee
- full ownership of the site after the engagement

### Compliance-Aware Execution

For a clinic in this category, aggressive marketing without compliance discipline is a liability. The program is built around compliant terminology, careful creative handling, and controlled use of automation.

## 10. Compliance and Risk Controls

This engagement is designed to grow the clinic while maintaining operational and reputational control.

Operating principles:

- No prohibited therapeutic claims, no guarantees, and no irresponsible promotional framing.
- No AI medical diagnosis and no AI-generated treatment outcomes.
- Human review of AI transcripts, ad copy, and sensitive website copy.
- Careful handling of before-and-after content and social proof.
- Neutral review and feedback mechanisms rather than manipulative testimonial collection.

This matters because the brief is not simply to generate attention. It is to build a growth system that the clinic can trust.
